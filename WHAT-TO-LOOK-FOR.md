# What to Look For — School Email Brief

A brief you can hand to another chatbot so it knows what counts as a relevant
school email for my family. **Treat the lists below as the *primary* signals, not
a hard whitelist.** If something is clearly about my kids, their school, or their
day, flag it — even if it doesn't match a keyword here. When in doubt, lean
toward surfacing it rather than dropping it.

## What counts as IMPORTANT (worth flagging me)

Flag it if the email has any of these:
- A **deadline, due date, or form** to submit
- A **survey or questionnaire** needing a parent response (even if "optional")
- A **payment or fee**
- A **schedule change** — early dismissal, cancellation, delay, closure
- A **request for a parent response or action**
- An **event needing prep** — field trip, spirit day, supplies, performance, conference
- Anything **directly affecting the kids' day**
- Anything about **special education / IEP / disability services / accommodations** (→ TJ)
- Any **digest or summary** from the school or district (ParentVUE, Cobb County, Garrison Mill digests) — **always flag these**, even if they look routine. I want to see all school digests.

## What's OK to SKIP (noise)

- Pure fundraising with no real deadline or urgency
- A duplicate reminder with no new information (same content I already saw today)
- Broad community / promotional blasts with nothing for me to do

Skip is the exception, not the default. If it's borderline, surface it.

---

## How to summarize one (when flagging)

Keep it short and scannable. Lead with **which child** and **how urgent**:
- **Action** — I have to *do* something (sign, pay, confirm, respond)
- **Heads up** — no action now, but I need to know (schedule change, upcoming event)
- **FYI** — good to know, no impact on the day

Then: one-sentence headline → any action items → relevant dates/deadlines →
what to put on the calendar → which school it came from.
